Daily Thinking

10 DAILY THINKINGS

Ten lines that capture how Seoyun thinks, learns, and moves.
01 · Picasso
“Action is the foundational key to all success.” 
Meaning: Success begins the moment you move, not when you finish planning.
Why it fits you: You constantly choose action over endless talking and overthinking.
02 · Benjamin Franklin
“Well done is better than well said.” 
Meaning: A finished piece of work is worth more than a perfectly explained idea.
Why it fits you: You are building a life where results quietly speak louder than words.
03 · Aristotle
“We are what we repeatedly do.” 
Meaning: Habits define identity more than one-time achievements.
Why it fits you: Your teaching, writing, and coding all grow out of long-term routines.
04 · Henri Matisse
“Creativity takes courage.” 
Meaning: Creating something new always exposes your inner world.
Why it fits you: Poems, essays, textbooks—each one is you choosing courage in public.
05 · Carol Dweck
“Becoming is better than being.” 
Meaning: The process of growing matters more than the label you have right now.
Why it fits you: You are more interested in your next version than in any fixed title.
06 · Joubert
“To teach is to learn twice.” 
Meaning: Teaching forces you to rebuild knowledge from the inside out.
Why it fits you: As both student and teacher, you turn every lesson into double learning.
07 · Unknown
“Clarity is the courage to be understood.” 
Meaning: Being clear means you are willing to be seen exactly as you are.
Why it fits you: Your grammar tables and materials always fight for clean structure.
08 · Earl Nightingale
“You become what you think about.” 
Meaning: The thoughts you revisit are shaping your future self every day.
Why it fits you: Your deep self-analysis and reflection are part of how you design yourself.
09 · Van Gogh
“Great things are done by a series of small things.” 
Meaning: Big outcomes are built from tiny, almost invisible pieces.
Why it fits you: Chunk lists, grammar tables, and steady content all live in this sentence.
10 · Gandhi
“The future depends on what you do today.” 
Meaning: Tomorrow is not created someday—it is created tonight.
Why it fits you: Your daily study and writing routines are already funding your future self.
